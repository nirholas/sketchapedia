Powder Lines, 2024

By nirholas  ·  Zermatt, January

The descent

Above the treeline the slope opens into a white canvas unmarked by tracks. Each turn throws a plume that hangs in the cold air for a fraction of a second before the wind erases it. The mountain keeps no memory of the line you drew.

The sequence below was captured at 120 fps and rendered frame-by-frame by the Sketchapedia generative layer. Each keyframe is content-addressed and cached at the edge — on repeat visits the CDN serves the whole run in under 200 ms.

How it works

A layout spec and hitmap are emitted by the LLM, then passed to the image model with ControlNet reference conditioning. The canvas displays the keyframe sequence while an invisible DOM overlay — real <input>, <button>, and ARIA roles — mirrors the hitmap so that clicks, keyboard navigation, and screen readers all land on the right target.

The trickiest problem is hitmap-to-pixel alignment. If the model draws the next-run button at (312, 480) but the hitmap says (298, 475), clicks misfire. The current approach enforces layout-first ControlNet constraints and runs a Florence-2 vision post-pass to correct drift before the frame is committed to the cache.
